What is this?

Councilmatic Chicago lets you keep tabs on what the Chicago City Council is doing.

The City Council consists of 50 aldermen, representing 50 wards in City of Chicago. They meet once a month to vote on all proposed loans, grants, bond issues, land acquisitions and sales, zoning changes, traffic control issues, mayoral appointees, and other financial appropriations.

Councilmatic Chicago lets you search, browse, subscribe and comment on everything the City Council has done since Jan 1st 2010. Some interesting searches include:

  • changes in zoning
  • what the city is paying out in settlements
  • committee appointments
  • birthdays

You can also see and follow all the legislation sponsored by your alderman or mayor (Bob Fioretti, Leslie Hairston, Rahm Emanuel).

If you want to keep tabs on an issue or topic, you can subscribe () to each ordinance or resolution, and if something new happens with it, you will get an e-mail notification.

One of the more unusual aspects of Chicago City Council is the volume of ordinances that are introduced and passed every month. Most of these are routine items like sign permits, damaged vehicle claims, sidewalk cafe approvals and honorifics.

To help sort through all of this, we have started classifying all legislation and tagging it appropriately. If something doesnâ€™t look like a routine piece of legislation, we tag it as Non-Routine to make it easier to discover.

Where do you get the data?

The data on this site is scraped once a week from from the Chicago City Council legislation site run by the Office of the City Clerk. Each piece of legislation provides a link to its source for reference.

Code

All of the code for this project is open source. You can find, view and fork it on github.

Technologies used:

  • Django 1.4 - web framework
  • PostGIS 1.5 - database
  • Legistar scraper - custom scraper by Forest Gregg
  • ebdata - EveryBlock address parser
  • Bootstrap - HTML/CSS layout
  • Google Maps
